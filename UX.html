UX/UI Contextmedia

Project Requirements


Problem: Doctors feel patients dont remember the vital information they are given during the examination. Patients feel as thought they can not remember the vital information given to them during the examination about their condition.


Solution: A white label website/mobile app with physician supported information regarding, cardiology, diabetes, neurology, and rheumetology.


Rapid prototyping for this project was done in Illustrator, Photoshop, and Invisionapp. The project was done through Lean methodology with problem/solution interviews, and usability testing drove several iterations of the project over 7 weeks.


Requirements of this project before production:

  • Contextmedia supplied videos
  • Assessment screening tools for patients of varying levels of conditions
  • Recommended products.
  • Check the next appointment.
  • Easy navigation and simplistic design for a demographic of patients ages 25-70.

5 member team consisting of 1 sales, 1 marketing, 2 product design, 1 web developer.


The screenshot is the final iteration of the project based on the solution interviews and usability test.

Project Challenges


Challenges facing the product design team:


  • Multi-featured website with attractive design while maintaining simplicity for older users.
  • A white label design for 1000's of doctors offices and medical facilities in 4 categories.(cardiology, diabetes, neurology and rheumatology) that requires minimal customization.
  • Satisfy 2 different clients with different wants and needs simulteneously

This was the first iteration of the website. Due to time constraints of the project we started designing before the first problem interviews. This iteration was laying out the features needed. (Not all screens present.)

Iteration One Results


Results of the first solution interview and usability test:


Positive

  • Large text and icons suitable for older users.
  • Positive and useful features.

Negative


  • Multiple navigation is confusing.
  • Navigation catagories were too vague.
  • No real connection to doctor/medical facility.
  • Physicians were cautious of the word "store."
  • Unclear mission/purpose.
  • Patients want to relate to other people.

Iteration Two Changes


  • Navigation is categorized much clearer.
  • Layout is suitable for text or visual-minded people.
  • Important features are given their own space.
  • Testimonials videos(NOT patient supplied videos) section added to give patients relateable material.

Iteration 2 Results

Solution Interview and Usability results.


Positive:


  • A much more attractive layout, yet still simple.
  • Better navigation catagories.
  • Clear mission and purpose.
  • Testimonials a welcome feature to patients.

Negative:


  • No connection to "My doctor."
  • Patients need even more personal connections to the website.
  • Doctors still unhappy with recommended products section.

Iteration 3


  • Added picture of facility doctor on front page.
  • Removed controversial appointment login from front page.

Iteration 3 Results


Solution Interview and Usability results.


Positive:


  • Starting to generate a desire to use.
  • Patients feel more connected to doctor and medical facility..
  • Positive reactions from both sides.

Negative:


  • Patients have an strong interest in the newest innovation in treatments and features available at their medical facility.
  • Reoccuring theme that patients are interested in anonymous interaction with other people with their condition.
  • Patients believe ability to check next appointment is a key feature.

Iteration 4 Final Changes


  • Ticker was added at the top with headlines about the newest innovation in treatments for the condition as well as information about new features at the patients medical facility.
  • Changed doctors picture and a name given to give testers the indication that this is a doctors real website.
  • Resources was expanded to include links to Support groups, online forums, vetted blog sources, new digital apps and reading material for patients.
  • Appointment restored to front page with the ability to ping the medical facility for an email with next appointment within the HIPAA guidelines.

Final Interation Features


  • Blue ticker was added at top after finding patients had strong interest in newest procedures and research for their condition as well as new services available at their medical facility.
  • Photo of doctor/doctors at the facility after patients expressed a need to know that the information was backed by their personal doctor.
  • Educational videos take center stage as Contextmedia has a huge database of vetted information to share with patients.
  • The ability to check their next appointment became one of the most important and valuable features from the patient point of view. It was decided that pinging the doctors office and sending a return email would be the best solution.
  • Assessment tools would allow new patients to become familiar with their condition and allow older patients to explore different way that their lifestyle could be making their condition worse
  • Patients expressed an interest in hearing how other people are dealing with their condition. The doctor can only treat the disease, but can not relate to the emotional impact that a terminal condition has on a person.
  • Resources evolved from a group of recommended products to fulfill the patients needs to connect with others anonymously through online communities and support groups and vetted blogs with more personal information.

All of these features were tested to comply with the needs and request of patients and physicians, not to increase communication between the two sides but to help create and environment where the communication is more efficient.

Educational Videos

The education menu allows patients to view videos in different categories relevant to their condition. Contextmedia host a library of 2500 videos for use through the office media stream as well as the new communication platform.


There is also the option to have a video sent to email to view later as with Contextmedia's current setup in the doctor waiting rooms across America.


Assessment Tools


Assessment features screening tools for new patients as well as patients with pre-existing conditions. Patients found the tools to helpful for newer patients who need to be brought up to speed on the basics of living with their illness, along with things that may prove beneficial or harmful with their current lifestyle. Assessment tools could also be used by family members to see if they too are at risk for developing the condition.


Resources


The resources category evolved the most from start to finish. After running into problem with the correct wording and the doctors negative views of pushing products to patients, the resources section became a place to fulfill the need of patients to connect with others who have the same conditions.


Through the patient solution interviews we were able to determine recommeded products were not as important to patients as connecting to other who have the same condition. However, we found that patients would prefer to do this anonymously, as most do not seek out friendships or face-to-face interactions. The resources page maintained a few products such as digital apps, and recommended reading such as cooks books, and nutritional guides, the real focus became centered around support groups that could be found on and offline, as well as popular blogs with vetted information.

Testimonials


The testimonial section was not one of the original features that was added in response to the patient solution interviews. Patients mentioned that it was a welcome feature to hear inspiring stories from other people who share the same trials and tribulations of conditions that they live with every day.


Patients were quick to add that although doctors are capable of treating the disease physically, they are sometimes not aware and cant relate to the emotional and mental impact that a terminal disease has on a patient.


There were concerns from a few office managers/doctors about these videos coming from their patients, but Contextmedia's video library contains testimonial videos from vetted sources which became an easy solution to overcome this problem.


Appointment


The ability to check the next scheduled appointment was one of the most valued features of the website according to the patient solution interviews. There were some concerns to HIPAA when it comes to the amount of patient information that is displayed on the web.


It was determined that patients would need to login with more than just a user name and password for added security, as well as, the end result would ping the doctors office to send the appointment information to the patients email.


Patients are not allowed to set up a new appointments through the website.

Mobile App


One of the requirements of the platform was the need for a mobile solution, which we planned to design the original website responsively. The overall color of the website was changed to purple to avoid the eye strain associated with a white background on a smaller screen and smaller font.


Conclusion


This first project in UX/UI taught me the importance of the craft. Web design has drastically changed from putting a webpage on the internet and hoping people will come and visit. There is a true science in the way people move through a website, what it is that keeps them there, and what it is that keeps them coming back.


The single most important thing that was learned about UX is that a majority of the time it is impossible to be both the user and the designer at the same time. As we learned from our problem and solution interviews, the two sides found value in different features that were available for separate reasons. But it was through these interviews and the usability test that we began to find a common ground amongst the patients, then amongst the physician, until we were finally able to settle on a common ground for most of the users coming to the website.


We as designers, would have never been able to pinpoint ourselves some of the things that would be considered valuable or the features that would set our website apart from every other website marketed to the terminally ill. For some of us, this was the first project that we've ever been associated that was not about promoting a product or creating a service, but was centered around the social impact of helping people and making the proper and relevant information available for everyone.
